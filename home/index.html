Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about scuba diving and our courses

The minimum age is 10 years old for the Junior Open Water Diver certification. From age 15, divers can earn the standard Open Water Diver certification. There's no maximum age as long as you're in good health.

Basic swimming skills are required. You'll need to swim 200 meters/yards (or 300 meters/yards in mask, fins and snorkel) without stopping, and float or tread water for 10 minutes. We offer swimming lessons if you need to improve your skills before starting the course.

The Open Water Diver course typically takes 3-4 days, including classroom sessions, confined water (pool) training, and open water dives. We offer flexible scheduling including weekend courses and eLearning options to complete the theory at your own pace.

When performed correctly following established guidelines and with proper training, scuba diving has an excellent safety record. Our courses emphasize safety procedures, and we maintain small class sizes to ensure proper supervision. Most diving accidents result from ignoring training or pushing limits.

The maximum recommended depth for Open Water Divers is 18 meters (60 feet). After completing the Advanced Open Water course, you'll be certified to dive to 30 meters (100 feet). Always dive within your training and experience limits.
